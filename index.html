About

I am a PhD student in economics working on topics related to inequality, belief formation, climate, and empirical policy analysis. I enjoy working with microdata, survey data, and applied econometrics.

My main tools are Stata, R, and Python. I like simple workflows, reproducible code, and empirical designs that make sense.

Research

Selected projects

  • Neural networks to forecast trade (with B. Meunier) Exploring when flexible models outperform classical forecasting tools.
  • Monetary policy transmission in a polarized labour market (with T. Zimmermann) Studying how policy shocks affect workers across the wage distribution.
  • Beliefs about inequality and redistribution PhD project on perceived inequality, mobility, fairness, and policy support.

Teaching

I have taught tutorials in econometrics and data analysis (Stata / R) and supervise empirical Bachelor and Master theses, especially on inequality, beliefs, or climate-related topics.
